Creating themes — color schemes for websites

Quick examples of common color schemes (monochrome, analogous, complementary, triadic, split, square/rectangle) and how to use them in UI.

Overview

The combination of colors used in the design of a website is called a color scheme. Colors set tone and mood — web developers use color theory and the color wheel to pick harmonious palettes.

Color theory basics

Primary colors are red, yellow and blue. Secondary colors (orange, purple, green) are mixes of primaries. Tertiary colors and tints form the 12-color color wheel. Designers use relative positions on the wheel to form schemes.

Common color schemes

There are seven commonly used schemes:

  • Monochromatic
  • Analogous
  • Complementary
  • Split complementary
  • Triadic
  • Square
  • Rectangle
Image source: moving.com — how to choose a color scheme

Using schemes on the web

Choose a scheme based on your site's purpose — e.g. complementary colors can highlight data visualizations; medical or informational sites often favor white backgrounds and muted accents. Keep UX first: avoid cognitive overload and use color to create clear visual hierarchy.
